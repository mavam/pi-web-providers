### **Mathematical Properties and Classifications**

$$42$$ is a composite number with rich structural properties, placing it in several notable integer sequences:

- **Pronic (or oblong) number**: A number of the form $$n(n+1)$$. $$42 = 6 \times 7$$, making it the 6th pronic number.
- **Abundant number**: The sum of its proper divisors exceeds itself: $$1 + 2 + 3 + 6 + 7 + 14 + 21 = 54 > 42$$.
- **Sphenic number**: A product of three distinct primes: $$42 = 2 \times 3 \times 7$$.
- **Catalan number**: $$42$$ is the 5th Catalan number, which counts various combinatorial structures such as the number of ways to triangulate a hexagon, or the number of non-crossing partitions of a set of 6 elements. The sequence begins: $$1, 1, 2, 5, 14, 42, \dots$$
- **Practical number**: Every integer less than $$42$$ can be expressed as a sum of distinct divisors of $$42$$.
- **Primary pseudoperfect number**: Satisfies $$\frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{7} + \frac{1}{42} = 1$$, a rare property shared by only a few numbers.

$$42$$ also appears in **magic cubes**. The smallest magic cube (a 3D arrangement of numbers where rows, columns, pillars, and diagonals sum to the same value) of order 3 has a magic constant of $$42$$, derived from the formula:

$$
M = \frac{n(n^3 + 1)}{2} = \frac{3(27 + 1)}{2} = 42
$$

In **Diophantine equations**, $$42$$ gained fame as one of the last numbers below 100 for which a solution to the sum-of-three-cubes problem was unknown:

$$
x^3 + y^3 + z^3 = 42
