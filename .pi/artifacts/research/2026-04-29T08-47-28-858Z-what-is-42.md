## Report
The number 42 is a natural number following 41 and preceding 43, notable for its mathematical properties and cultural significance.

Mathematically, 42 is a pronic number (the product of two consecutive integers, $$6 \times 7$$), an abundant number (the sum of its proper divisors exceeds itself), a sphenic number (the product of three distinct prime numbers: $$2 \times 3 \times 7$$), and a Catalan number. It is also the magic constant of the smallest non-trivial $$3 \times 3 \times 3$$ magic cube, where every row, column, corridor, and diagonal through the center sums to 42. Additionally, 42 can be expressed as the sum of three cubes:

$$
(80,435,758,145,817,515)^3 + (12,602,123,297,335,631)^3 + (-80,538,738,812,075,974)^3 = 42
$$

It is also the sum of the first three odd powers of 2:

$$
2^1 + 2^3 + 2^5 = 2 + 8 + 32 = 42
$$

and the sum of the first two nonzero powers of 6:

$$
6^1 + 6^2 = 6 + 36 = 42
$$

In science and nature, rainbows appear at an angle of 42° relative to the light source, and 42 is the expansion rate of the universe in miles per second per megaparsec (though this value is approximate and subject to ongoing research).

Culturally, 42 is famously known as "the Answer to the Ultimate Question of Life, the Universe, and Everything" in Douglas Adams's *The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy*. This answer was calculated by the supercomputer Deep Thought after 7.5 million years, though the actual question remains unknown. Adams stated he chose 42 arbitrarily as a joke, but the number has since inspired numerous references in technology, media, and pop culture—including Google's calculator easter egg for the phrase "the answer to the ultimate question of life, the universe, and everything."

In sports, 42 is the retired jersey number of Jackie Robinson in Major League Baseball, honored league-wide since 1997. It is worn by all uniformed personnel on Jackie Robinson Day (April 15) each year.
